# Convexity Alpha Pipeline — Cohort-01 Selection (2024-Q4)

**Track:** Trend-with-stops (convex). Vol-expansion deferred (no VIX/options data in the lakes).
**Pipeline:** `src/convexity_pipeline/` (Stages 0–4). Runs alongside the untouched `src/alpha_pipeline/`.
**Runner:** `scripts/research/convexity_alpha_runner.py`.
**Registry source:** `data/alpha_registry/alpha_registry_v2.xlsx`, sheet `Registry`.

## Selection filter

Per implementation plan Step 3, candidates were filtered on:
`Cat# = 1` AND `Expected Payoff Shape = convex` AND `Convexity Track ∈ {trend, both}` AND
`Priority` starts `T1` AND `Pipeline Stage = S0`. Sixteen rows matched. We preferred
explicit, fully-specified rule sets (so the signal can be coded without discretion) and a
spread of asset classes and trade-engines.

### Why these five
- **Engine diversity:** recursive smoother (4), channel breakout w/ trailing stop (17, 5),
  regression-channel breakout (16), and a long-horizon MA regime filter (8).
- **Asset diversity:** crypto daily (4, 17), energy futures intraday (5), equity ETFs daily (16, 8).
- **Explicit rules:** each has a closed-form entry/exit codable without discretion (see the
  per-candidate hypothesis files under `docs/research/hypotheses/`).

### Documented deviations from the plan's preferred list
The plan suggested *Continuation Index, Ultimate Channels, Unger Crude SuperTrend, Kaufman channel,
Pinsky Three Metals*. Four are used as-is. **Three Metals Session Bias (ID 15) was replaced by the
MA Black Swan Filter (ID 8)** because:
1. Three Metals needs **intraday session-timing** across GC/SI/HG (long overnight gold, long daytime
   copper). The Phase-1 long/flat daily/60-min adapter cannot faithfully represent session legs.
2. **GC has no back-adjusted continuous artifact**, and the futures DuckDB lake is **locked** by the
   live ingestion process (`refresh_dated_futures_minute`, PID observed at build time), so GC/SI
   continuous series were unavailable.
3. The MA Black Swan Filter (ID 8) is an explicit-rule, T1, convex-trend candidate with full ETF
   history (2005→) — a clean substitute that also broadens the equity-ETF leg.

**Crude SuperTrend runs on CL only** (not CL+NG): the NG/SI front-month parquet artifacts are
**not back-adjusted** (roll gaps up to ~47% inject spurious fat tails — measured 60-min return skew
+2.84 with 200 bars > 10% move), whereas CL's institutional continuous series is back-adjusted and
clean (60-min return skew ≈ −0.08). Running on the contaminated NG series produced a spurious
aggregate skew of −4.3; CL-only gives the genuine convex profile (skew +1.55).

## Hypothesized cross-candidate correlation

| Pair | Expected correlation | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 4 ↔ 17 | **High** | Same crypto universe, both long-trend; CI is always-in, UC is selective breakout |
| 16 ↔ 8 | **High** | Overlapping equity-ETF universe, both long/flat trend on equities |
| 5 ↔ {others} | **Low** | Independent asset (crude oil), independent clock (60-min) |
| {4,17} ↔ {16,8} | **Low–moderate** | Crypto vs equities; different macro drivers |

This is intentional: the cohort tests whether the pipeline (a) promotes the genuinely convex
crypto/futures trend alphas and (b) correctly *rejects* equity long/flat strategies that look
trend-like but are not convex at the per-bar level. See the results doc for the outcome.
